The Plot

A Silent Voice a fictional story that revolves around a Japanese teenager named Shoya Ishida who itends to commit suicide. As he changes his mind last minute, he recalls his elementary school days that have led him to this point.

On an average day, a new student named Shoko Nishimiya transferred to his school, informing the class that she was deaf. She tried to communicate with the class by writing in a notebook but she ends up annoying Shoya and his close friends, so they start to bully her. They threw Shoko's books in the fountain, they crushed numerous of her hearing aids and yelled directly in her ear. When word of the bullying reached to the principal, Shoya was singled out as a culprit in the bullying. (Although many others did as well) Soon, the class bullying was directed at Shoya instead. Shoya ends up blaming Shoko, ending up in a physical fight between the 2. Shoko ended up transferring to another school in a few days. Shoya was blamed for her moving as well. He later finds Shoko's notebook, which she left behind, and Shoya decides to keep it.

Now in high school, Shoya was a social reject. He was known for bullying a deaf kid and making her transfer to another school. He blocks out the faces of people around them, unable to look them in the eye. Soon he meets another loner named Tomohiro Nagatsuka. They soon become very close friends.

One day, Shoya coincidentally meets Shoko once again while walking by. He walks into the Sign Language Center hoping to find her again. He hoped to made amends by giving Shoko her notebook back, following by the fact that he studied sign language. Thankfully they both made amends and Shoko slowly forgives him over time.

Throughout Shoya's time in high school, he meets many of his old classmates from Elementary School. Trying to make amends with all of them. Everything was going smoothly...

Until...

Shoko was trying to commit suicide. Under the pressure of her grandmother recently passing and her blaming herself for Shoya's social rejection. In tears, Shoya sucessfully pulls Shoko up from the ledge. But instead, he falls into the river and goes into a coma.

One night, Shoko had a dream about recieving a farewell visit from Shoya. In tears, she runs to the bridge where they fed koi and collaspes into tears. Coincidentally, Shoya awakens from his coma and stumbles upon Shoko on the bridge. He apologizes to her about how he treated her in Elementary school and how nothing is her fault. Continuing to ask Shoko is she could help him continue to live. Crying, Shoyo is relieved he has finally obtained forgiveness and redemption,

The End.

Conclusion

Throughout Shoya's journey of affliction and drama he has learned many things. He finds out how much his new friends care for them and they reconcile. He was taught the meaning of forgiveness and challenge. Shoya is now able to live a (somewhat) average life.
